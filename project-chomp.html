Overview

Have you ever sat down one night for dinner and was not in any mood to cook? Or what about when you order Chipotle for the 10th time and ask yourself, is this all there is? Well, CHOMP is the app for you. I participated in a UX/UI bootcamp led by the Creative + User Experience Team at Duke University where we were tasked with this prompt, and learned about content and design principles as we worked out a way to solve our common problem. Here's my own take on the bootcamp's prompt.

The Problem

After a year of the pandemic, we're all tired of cooking. We need to find a way to provide more dining options and promote local restaurants.

Our first step as a group was to understand what our stakeholders envisioned for this product. We came up with key questions to include in our interview, some of which are below:

  • How do you currently go about choosing where to eat?
  • What do you like and disliked about food delivery apps you currently use?
  • What information would you like to have when choosing where to go?
  • For this mobile app, how would you define success?

Content Analysis

After going through the answers from the stakeholder interview, we compiled words and phrases that stood out to us and sorted them into key categories.

Eating Habits

"Typically, I guess the kids tell me what they want and I go and get what they want. But, when I’m by myself, it’s mostly whether or not I feel like soup or like fries. So, I let my stomach decide. But, I wish I was more adventurous because I only go to the same places. I go to McDonald’s or Panera bread and that’s it."

"I have my partner, so I have to take into consideration someone else’s opinions. But, I think we probably have about 10 places that we rotate through take-out from."

Finding Food

"There are a lot of places I want to go that I see on Instagram."

"If I know it, I’ll go there. If I don’t know it, I’m not searching for it. "

"I use GrubHub because they have those bigger categories like Asian and they list the restaurants. And then I peruse the different restaurant menus."

Success Criteria

"It turns me on to a new place because I feel like I’m pretty knowledgeable with my options of where to eat around here. So it would be nice - a delightful outcome - I find out about a new restaurant that I haven’t tried yet and it’s really good. "

" It will tell me where to eat and I will actually like the place it selects."

The Design Process

To start our design, I sorted the features the stakeholders mentioned into categories that fit most appropriately. Then, I made wireframes to think about the best layouts for the app, and looked to Dribbble and Behance for inspiration.

Final Design

Sticking with the warm color palette we chose in the bootcamp, I chose the typography to give the app a more playful feel to match my proposed logo.

Reflection

Participating in this bootcamp was an amazing experience. I am so thankful to have the experience to learn about the components of UX/UI design from talented individuals, as well as the opportunity to work with others interested in the same field. Working on CHOMP in particular was a really fun experience. I spent a lot of time browsing sites such as Behance and Dribbble for inspiration, and this certainly challenged my conceptions of UI design. I found that the most useful part of the process was the content analysis. Sorting information into cards that group together common categories was very helpful for me to organize my own information. I found myself going back to this list and creating new ones, understanding what the flow of information should be and how to make it the easiest for the user. It is definitely going to be a practice I continue in future projects.
