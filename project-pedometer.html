HEALTH AND WELLNESS APPS

Survey of 3 Popular Pedometer Apps to determine what are the qualities of good UX/UI in the health and wellness field.

OVERVIEW

To determine what makes a good health and fitness app, I wanted to take a 3-pronged approach: compile app store feedback, user research, and use Figma to do a little bit of redesigning.

TEAM

UX Researcher, UI Designer

TIMELINE

2 weeks

BACKGROUND | There are more than 318,000 health and wellness apps currently in the app store, with 200+ being added each day. Especially due to COVID, more and more of us have turned to the virtual world to guide us on our health journeys. Home gym and workout equipment has had skyrocketing sales as people aim to improve or create healthy habits at home. One popular and easy way of keeping healthy is by using a habit tracker app.

With many on the market, I wanted to look at 3 pedometer apps-Pacer, StepsApp, Pedometer++-and determine what draws user attention and retains users.

A pedometer itself is a relatively simple concept, and on most iPhones, users already have a built-in health app that does just that. What I wanted to know was what additional features, interface, etc, gives an app the edge to attract users in.

USER RESEARCH | Given my initial review of the app and comparing them to apple’s health app, I conducted 6 in-depth interviews with people of varying ages (19-55) and of varying exercise habits (low-high) for their opinions of each app.

General:

  • Have you used health and fitness apps before?
  • How active would you say you are?
  • What is the biggest factor behind exercising for you?
  • Would you ever want to download a habit-tracker app?
  • What components would you expect to see in a step-counter app?

App-Specific:

  • Are you able to find all of the expected components of a habit-tracker app?
  • What are your thoughts (positive and negative) about these apps?

KEY TAKEAWAYS

  • An attracting interface can greatly influence a user
  • Too many features != More attractive
  • Buttons should be intuitive - don’t make your users play hide and seek

AUDIENCE | Based on the users’ responses, I was able to identify 3 different users, 1 for each app. Pedometer++ attracted more of an older, less tech-savvy audience. StepsApp seemed more towards those who wanted to track a little bit more than their steps and could use a little extra motivation. Pacer was for those who were more dedicated to fitness, specifically those who looked for the most out of their apps.

IMPROVING UPON APP DESIGN | I took to Figma to redesign the landing pages of the 3 apps based on my takeaways from the user research

PACER

Overall, Pacer has many positive features and is widely-liked by its users. During my research, I found that in their initial impressions of the app, Pacer's biggest issue was a confusing user-flow.

Pacer Revisions

StepsApp

StepsApp was the app seen most favorably through the research. Its sleek and minimalistic appearance is quite appealing to a variety of groups. Based on the strengths of the other apps, I proposed some minor suggestions.

StepsApp Revisions

Pedometer++

Pedometer++ was the easiest app to follow in the three. While it's simpleness was a positive, some users felt it didn't have enough features to draw them towards it. One of its biggest strengths were in the awards, which are clever ways of motivating its users.
